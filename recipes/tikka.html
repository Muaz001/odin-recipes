Chicken Tikka

Description

Chicken tikka is a beloved dish in Indian cuisine, featuring succulent pieces of chicken marinated in a blend of yogurt and spices. Typically cooked in a tandoor oven for a smoky char, the result is tender, flavorful chicken with a vibrant golden hue.

Served as an appetizer or part of a meal, chicken tikka is enjoyed for its bold flavors and aromatic appeal, often accompanied by cooling yogurt sauces and warm naan bread, making it a timeless favorite among food enthusiasts worldwide.

Ingredients

  • 500 grams boneless chicken, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • 1 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 tablespoons ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tablespoon lemon juice
  • 2 teaspoons ground cumin
  • 2 teaspoons ground coriander
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon paprika or Kashmiri red chili powder (for color)
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil or melted ghee, for marinating
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil or melted ghee, for grilling
  • Fresh coriander leaves (cilantro), for garnish
  • Lemon wedges, for serving
    • Optional ingredients for extra flavor:
  • 1 teaspoon ground fenugreek (kasuri methi)
  • 1 teaspoon chaat masala
  • 1 teaspoon ground red chili powder (for extra heat)
    • Feel free to adjust the spices according to your taste preferences!

Steps

  1. Marinate the Chicken:
    1. In a large bowl, combine yogurt, ginger-garlic paste, lemon juice, ground cumin, ground coriander, turmeric powder, paprika or Kashmiri red chili powder, ground black pepper, garam masala, and salt. Mix well to form a smooth marinade.
    2. Add the bite-sized chicken pieces to the marinade, ensuring they are well coated. Cover the bowl and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight, to allow the flavors to meld and the chicken to tenderize.
  2. Prepare the Skewers:
    1. If using wooden skewers, soak them in water for about 30 minutes to prevent them from burning during grilling.
    2. Thread the marinated chicken pieces onto the skewers, leaving a little space between each piece for even cooking.
  3. Preheat the Grill:
    1. Preheat your grill to medium-high heat. If using an oven, preheat it to the highest broil setting.
  4. Grill the Chicken:
    1. Brush the grill grates with oil to prevent sticking.
    2. Place the chicken skewers on the preheated grill and cook for about 10-12 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and has a charred, slightly crispy exterior. If using an oven, place the skewers on a baking sheet lined with aluminum foil and broil for 10-12 minutes, flipping halfway through.
  5. Serve:
    1. Once cooked, remove the chicken tikka skewers from the grill or oven.
    2. Garnish with fresh coriander leaves (cilantro) and serve hot with lemon wedges on the side.
    Chicken tikka is typically enjoyed as an appetizer or served with naan bread, rice, or a side salad. Enjoy the delicious flavors of homemade chicken tikka!
